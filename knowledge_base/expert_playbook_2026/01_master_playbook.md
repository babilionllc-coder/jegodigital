## PART A — 5 MOVES TO INCREASE COLD EMAIL REPLY RATE

### 1. Implement advanced personalization snippets
Move beyond `{{firstName}}`. Include a custom one-liner tied to the prospect's specific listing or a concrete observation about their lead-flow gap (e.g. "noticed your Facebook page has 4 listings but no WhatsApp CTA"). **Woodpecker data** shows advanced personalization roughly doubles reply rates — prospects increasingly reject generic robot-like templates and reward human-to-human signal.

### 2. Personalize every subject line
Incorporate the prospect's domain or a specific agency detail in the subject line. Personalized subjects lift open rates 10% — the mandatory first gate to any reply. Pair with JegoDigital's existing "1-3 word subjects" rule: e.g. `{{company}} — visibilidad`, not just `Tu competencia`.

### 3. Standardize a 3-step follow-up sequence
Ensure EVERY campaign has 2–3 strategic follow-ups after Step 1. This frequency consistently produces the highest reply rates — enough persistence without triggering deliverability penalties. Current JegoDigital CLAUDE.md already mandates 5-step sequences; verify every active campaign actually has Steps 2–5 built (42% of replies come from follow-ups per `cold-email-sequences-2026.md`).

### 4. Execute lean micro-campaigns
Abandon 500-lead blasts. Build tight segments — e.g. the 172+ AMPI Cancún partners — and tailor copy to that specific audience's professional standards and pain. Small, targeted segments dramatically outperform mass-blast because you can reference their world by name.

### 5. Align the "Trojan Horse" with AMPI's "digitalization" narrative
AMPI Cancún leadership has positioned 2026 around industry digitalization. Reframe the free Service-1 install as "supporting the digitalization of the sector" rather than "free AI setup." This shifts Alex's perceived role from hands-on techie to respected industry consultant — easier reply for a broker who doesn't want to be seen buying "tools" but DOES want to be seen modernizing.

## PART B — 5 MOVES TO INCREASE CLOSE RATE + REVENUE/CLIENT

### 6. Open every Calendly call with "the Why Conversation"
Do not pitch features for the first 10 minutes. Ask: *"Why lead capture now? Why are you talking to us specifically?"* This uncovers motivation and budget elasticity. Shifts Alex from "hands-on techie" to "respected consultant" and reliably enables closes at 2x the client's stated initial budget. Chris Do frame — the core lever.

### 7. Anchor proposals with three tiers
Never present a single price. Offer 3 tiers — e.g. **Lead Capture** (current bundle) / **Full Automation** (+SEO+AEO+Social) / **Strategic Growth** (add developer contract / performance-based). Clients reliably pick the middle or top option when given "choice of yeses." Hormozi + Brennan Dunn consensus. This is the #1 pricing change to make before the next discovery call.

### 8. Transition from one-time setups to "peace of mind" retainers
Replace one-time fees with ongoing support/strategy retainers. Jonathan Stark's model shows retainers can yield effective hourly rates of ~$1,000 USD by delivering 150% perceived value. For JegoDigital this means framing Service-1 as a 12-month partnership with quarterly strategic reviews, not a "one and done" install.

### 9. Price outcomes, not hours
Stop quoting "$4,900 MXN/mo for SEO." Quote the outcome: "#1 Google Maps in your ZIP + 3x qualified lead flow in 90 days." Brennan Dunn / Nick Disabato productized-consulting frame — charge the value of the solved problem, not the ingredient list. This reframes MXN price objections ("es caro") into ROI conversations.

### 10. Position every high-tier offer under the AMPI "digitalization" umbrella
Frame Pack Dominación and developer contracts as the "primary vehicle for the professionalization of Mexican real estate." This elevates Alex above peso-for-peso competitors and justifies USD-denominated pricing for developer work. Use AMPI's own words in proposal preambles.

## BLUNT AUDIT (from the notebook sources)

Two structural issues the playbook flags:

- **The "9 productized services" list is creating fogginess.** Prospects don't want a menu — they want to buy the conversation and the "Why." Cold email should sell the call, not the features. Consider collapsing customer-facing marketing to 3 outcomes: (a) more leads 24/7, (b) #1 on search + AI, (c) sell without being there. Keep the 9-service architecture internal.
- **$3K–$20K MXN/mo is a commodity trap.** At the top of that range Alex can serve ~30 clients and cap at ~$226K USD. To hit $1M, use moves 6–10 to uncover $100K+ MXN problems and anchor at 5–10x current rates with developer contracts and performance-based deals.
